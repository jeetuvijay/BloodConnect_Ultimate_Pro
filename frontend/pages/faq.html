For Recipients

Finding blood donors

Generally, you must be at least 18 years old, weigh at least 110 pounds, and be in good health. Some medical conditions or medications may temporarily or permanently prevent donation. Please consult with our medical team for specific eligibility requirements.

Whole blood donors can donate every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donors can donate every 7 days, up to 24 times per year. Plasma donors can donate twice weekly, with at least 48 hours between donations.

Get plenty of sleep, eat a healthy meal, drink extra fluids, and avoid fatty foods. Bring your ID, wear comfortable clothing, and be prepared to stay for about 1-2 hours total.

Most donors feel only a brief pinch when the needle is inserted. The actual donation process is painless. Some donors experience mild dizziness or bruising afterward, but these effects are usually temporary.

Visit our Hospital Registration page and provide your facility's information, including license details, contact information, and blood bank credentials. Our team will verify your information and approve your account within 24-48 hours.

In emergency situations, log into your hospital dashboard and submit an urgent blood request with the required blood type, quantity, and urgency level. Our system will immediately notify compatible donors in your area and coordinate the donation process.

Hospitals can search for available donors through their dashboard. For emergency situations, contact your local hospital's blood bank directly. They can access our network to find compatible donors quickly.

Blood type compatibility depends on the ABO and Rh systems. For example, O- is the universal donor, while AB+ can receive from any type. Our system automatically matches compatible donors based on medical standards.

Log into your account and navigate to your profile settings. You can update your contact information, medical history, and donation preferences. Keep your information current to ensure accurate matching.
